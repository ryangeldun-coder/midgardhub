6. Navigation

Use the navigation to find NPCs and monsters, as well as the (in theory) best route to get there. All you have to do is follow the arrows down the floor.

To get there, click after using “Move” to travel there manually, or pay for the service using various items, which will teleport you directly to the selected map:

7. Channels

Each map is divided into several zones: the ‘current’ zone, followed by zones or channels A, B, C and D. Depending on the number of players in these zones, you can decide to switch between them. This device is available on every map near a warp.

Important note: When you are in one of these zones, all warps are disabled. To return to the ‘global’ or shared zone, you must select ‘current’ as shown below; as Zone A was previously selected, it does not appear in the list of suggestions. Once you have been teleported to the current/global channel, the warps will be available again.

7. Hidden World

In addition to the five zones, including the so-called ‘current’ zone, there are also two PvP zones, Y and Z, which can be accessed via the NPC who looks like a seated Novice:

Important note: unlike the global zones A, B, C and D, you cannot teleport once you are inside these PvP zones.

The benefits of these PvP zones are:

  • A greater number of monsters
  • Faster respawn times
  • 10% EXP boost (can be activated)*
  • 10% drop rate boost (can be activated)*

To get there, you’ll need a key, which can be obtained via the Cash Shop and is valid for your character; it cannot be placed in storage:

* Once the box has been opened, you can use the item as an activatable boost that increases experience and drop rates by 10% for 12 hours.

To leave this area, simply return to the character selection screen, which will take you straight to your save point when you return to the game.

Note: There is a daily reset of the PvP maps at 2.00 am (CEST); your character will then be teleported to one of the non-PvP areas and will continue hunting as before, but without the 10% EXP/drop bonus.

8. Achievements

Rewards based on challenges you’ve completed and those still to come are available in the game; these often take the form of Increase Agi or Blessing buffs, but may also include cosmetic titles or items:

Simply complete the challenges and then click the “Claim” button to receive your rewards.

On most maps, there is a treasure; you can search for it and earn Zenys after claiming your reward from this menu.

9. Beginning

First of all, if you struggle to log into your account, please do check that you are not having any capital letters in your email as this is case sensitive.

It has been a common mistake for people having connection issues, now you know !

The start is very different from what you might have experienced on other servers, and the directions are fairly clear – you just need to follow the arrows to go from one NPC to another and do what they ask. You won’t have to grind job levels to change class; you’ll pick them up as you go through the tutorial quest.

Do remember to allocate your skill points, though, or you’ll get stuck and the NPC won’t let you change class ! Next, all you need to do is go upstairs and head to the room with the name of the class you want to play. There’s no need for a job-change quest like in the old days.

10. Affixes

Affixes are an essential part of the game; getting the piece of equipment with the best affixes for your class is the way to go.

The list of affixes is available here:https://midgardhub.com/tools/affix

When an item with one or more affixes drops to the ground, it will be surrounded by a coloured halo corresponding to the number of affixes:

  • blue for 1
  • yellow for 2
  • purple for 3
  • green for 4 (to be checked)
  • Red for 5 (to be checked)

Notes:

- Max HP is often prioritised to avoid dying too early

- HIT and FLEE are quite important for melee classes

- Max SP for classes that use skills

- Or Variable Casting Time for casters (mages and acolytes)

13. Main Quest (MQ)

Throughout your adventure on the server, there’s a main quest available which offers a change from the XP grind and also allows you to gain experience. Most of these are still ‘FedEx’-style quests, but they do provide a welcome break.

There are level requirements, and you won’t be able to complete everything in one go (for example, level 20, level 40, etc.). The main quest continues as new patches are released. Note: During the open beta, doing all the main quest was about 45% at level 49.

14. Auto Mods

14.1. Auto Hunt

This is a feature that allows your character to hunt autonomously based on a whole host of parameters, which I won’t go into here as they’re fairly self-explanatory.

Once auto-hunt has been set up, simply click on the twin swords so that they turn blue, indicating that auto-hunt is activated. Your character will then begin to explore the map according to your settings.

The Monsters section is reset between each map change, so if you decide to exclude any monsters, you’ll need to redo that selection only.

Note: in the Skills tab, don’t forget that there are 3 pages:

You can configure a key to enable or disable the auto-hunt.

Note that auto-hunt does not work in instances or in WoE.

14.2. Auto Pot

To be used during manual hunting; the settings for this auto-pot remain the same as those set in auto-hunt. Very useful when managing your HP/SP isn’t a priority.

You can assign a key to toggle the mode on and off.

15. Early Game

Mage/Range: Flora, Parasite, Grove, Steel Chonchon…

Melee: There are two types: those that can use AoE and those that can’t:

  • Non-AoE: Spore, Bigfoot, Coco, Thara, Sohee, Orc Warrior/Lady, Argiope…
  • AoE: Poison Spore, Wolf, Goblin…

Note to be verified: Experience on Zero does not appear to be scaled with level as expected in Renewal; in other words, a monster will always grant the same amount of experience regardless of your level, so certain ranged classes or even groups can take full advantage of this.

16. Group Leveling

A group experience bonus is available and, according to the latest information, it works as follows:

This means that a team, or a group where everyone plays their part, will perform relatively better than if you’re playing solo; the same applies to auto-hunting if everyone manages to get by on their own.

Depending on the group’s composition and provided auto-hunt is not used, a group of 10–12 players can easily target leveling areas that are significantly tougher than those they would face whilst soloing or using auto-hunt.

Example: When soloing, certain classes will head to the Orcs around levels 30–35; it is highly likely that a good group could venture there as early as level 25.

A duo or trio consisting of a mobber, a damage dealer (DD) and a support character is also very effective, depending on the zone they’re in.

Example: If you’re just a tad short of HIT to maintain consistent damage output, there’s nothing better than a duo with an Acolyte for a Blessing and Increase Agi. If the support character has Pneuma, a zone like the Parasites will be a piece of cake.

17. Leveling per Class

17.1. Mage

As you can reset your stats and skills for free up to level 40, you can simply start off in melee mode right from the start until you get Earth Spike to level 1. Head to Culvert and follow the path to get Fire Bolt to level 10. Reset your stats and max out INT.

Head for Ant Hell, the aim being to reach level 20–30 and be able to one-shot the Wolves. Use the lowest possible level with Fire Bolt. Once you’re comfortable, you can have a go at the Steel Chonchons with Earth Spike level 5.

Other possibilities, amongst many others no doubt, include the Parasites, the Groves, the Argiopes… Note that auto-hunt won’t be your friend on these final maps.

My experience is the following: Get 2 level jobs on Spore, get Fire Bolt level 2 and stay on Spore until Earth Spike is 5 then move to Steel Chonchon and stay there until the level 50 base 50 job. Very chill… 🙂

17.2. Thief

Culvert / (Poison) Spore > Bigfoot > Thara Frog / Coco > Orc Warrior/Lady

Your strength, your dodging, your weakness… no area-of-effect spells. You’ll have to bash the monsters one by one.

17.3. Swordman

Culvert / (Poison) Spore > Bigfoot > Thara Frog / Coco > Orc Warrior/Lady

Your strength, your tankiness. You’ll have to bash the monsters one by one; Bash is your best friend, especially with the stun passive. Alternatives using Magnum Break may be worth considering.

17.4. Archer

Culvert / (Poison) Spore > Bigfoot > Thara Frog / Coco > Orc Warrior/Lady

Just like the Thief/Swordman, except that you’re a ranged class, which gives you a few alternatives such as Flora or Parasites. There might be a way to make use of Arrow Shower and MTs… it’s up to you to try it out 👍

17.5. Merchant

Culvert / (Poison) Spore > Bigfoot > Thara Frog / Coco > Orc Warrior/Lady > Goblin

With plenty of SP, Cart Revolution can be a formidable ally, especially towards the end of levelling, when it starts to take much longer.

17.6. Acolyte

As you can reset your stats (and skills) for free up to level 40, don’t worry too much about it – in the early game, you can simply go for a melee build.

The Acolyte will follow much the same path as the other melee classes. As the heal bomb is rather poor, I’d advise going for a battle build before level 40; after that, you’ll need to decide on your future path and probably find a duo or a group if you’re going down the support route.

21. Mission Board

Apart from the main quest, I wouldn’t recommend doing the side quests, as they involve relatively interesting tasks but offer rewards that are only average at best (HP/SP potions).

22. RODEX

Aka RO Delivery Express is a mail system built into the UI. Indirect communication with players can go through in the case of “Leave Offer” messages but this is also the way the game will send you rewards or purchases.

The official mails sent by the system will be stored in the “Notice” tab, while interaction with other players will be set in the “Normal” tab.

Up to and including level 40, you can reset your stats and skills for free. Above level 40, if you want to reset either of these, it will cost you 1 Zelstar per additional level: if you are level 80, you will need 40 Zelstars.

A word of advice: don’t mess up, or else create several characters with different builds.

26. Inn Exp Boost

Each inn has its own opening hours; if you rest at the inn during the relevant times, you will receive a +10% EXP bonus for 30 minutes.

Note: The server times are in UTC, so add 2 hours in summer and 1 hour in winter.

Example: 14:00 is 16:00 during summer time, otherwise it is 15:00 during winter time.

27. Earn Zenys

First tip: make sure you have a Merchant with Discount and Overcharge set to 10 on your account; this will allow them to take items directly from the Kafra and sell them at a good price. Bear in mind that some items will likely be in high demand amongst players, and it’ll be more profitable to sell them to players rather than to NPCs.

Lots of monsters drop Fly Wings; you’ll probably end up with more than you need, but they sell very, very well to NPCs.

As for loot in general, don’t hesitate to pick everything up, many items are very useful. Selling them to NPCs or other players will allow you to afford purchases such as HP/SP consumables or ammunition.

Oridecon/Elunium ores (whether refined or not) are sure to be an essential resource, particularly as there’s no ‘safe refine’ option, so you’re going to need a looooooot of them🎉.
