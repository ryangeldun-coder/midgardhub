Beginner's Guide

Ragnarok Zero Global — Getting Started

New to Ragnarok Zero? This guide covers everything you need to know to get started — class selection, early leveling strategy, core game systems, and the most common beginner mistakes. Ragnarok Zero Global is based on the TWRo Zero Taiwan server.

What is Ragnarok Zero Global?

Ragnarok Zero Global is a remastered version of the classic Korean MMORPG Ragnarok Online, developed by Gravity. Unlike the original RO, Ragnarok Zero features updated graphics, a reworked EXP curve, and modern quality-of-life improvements while preserving the core gameplay — class-based progression, open-world exploration, player-driven economy, and deep build customization.

The Global server (ROZ Global) is based on the TWRo Zero Taiwanese server, which is the most feature-complete version of RO Zero. The game is played in a 2.5D isometric perspective with sprite-based graphics — the iconic Ragnarok art style — but with higher resolution, smoother animations, and expanded content.

Key differences from classic RO: no WoE (War of Emperium) in the current content cycle, a more structured main quest line, the Activation System for starred weapon upgrades, Memorial Dungeons for instanced party content, and Random Options on dropped equipment.

Choose Your Class

You start as a Novice and choose your first job at level 10. Every class has two second-job paths. Pick based on your preferred playstyle — you can always make an alt later.

Swordsman
Knight / Crusader

High HP and defense. Knights are melee powerhouses, Crusaders mix offense with party support. Best first class for new players — forgiving playstyle and great for solo leveling.

Mage
Wizard / Sage

Massive AoE magic damage. Wizards delete entire screens of monsters. Sage brings utility and Endow scrolls. Gear-dependent but rewarding once equipped.

Archer
Hunter / Bard / Dancer

High single-target damage with high flee. Hunters excel at solo farming with traps. Bard and Dancer provide unique party support songs. Safe and versatile.

Thief
Assassin / Rogue

Fastest killer with Crit builds. Assassins deal burst damage with dual-wielding. Rogues excel at AFK farming with auto-steal. High flee makes them tanky against physical mobs.

Merchant
Blacksmith / Alchemist

Economy powerhouses. Blacksmiths forge weapons and deal huge damage with Mammonite. Alchemists brew potions and summon homunculi. Essential for any serious zeny-maker.

Acolyte
Priest / Monk

Priests heal and buff parties — the backbone of any group. Monks are solo powerhouses with Combo and Asura Strike. Priest is arguably the most valuable class for party play.

Recommended for beginners: Swordsman (forgiving), Hunter (safe farming), or Priest (always wanted in parties). Avoid Wizard and Alchemist as a first character — they require significant investment to perform well.

Early Game Tips

Always do Main Quests first
The Main Quest chain (MQ) gives massive EXP and introduces you to the map. Follow it until you hit a level wall, then grind briefly and continue.
Create a Merchant alt immediately
Even at level 1, a Merchant can set up a vending stall. Overcharge (+24% NPC sell) and Discount (-24% NPC buy) save significant zeny over time.
Check drop value before vendoring
Some seemingly worthless drops are crafting materials worth hundreds of thousands of zeny on the player market. Always check prices first.
Don't spend zeny on gear before Base Level 50
You'll outlevel equipment quickly in the early game. Use quest rewards and monster drops until around level 50-60 before investing heavily in gear.
Complete Critura Academy early
The Critura Academy quest chain in Izlude gives great starter EXP and introduces core game systems. Complete it before heading to the field.
Join a guild
Guild buffs provide significant stat boosts. Even a small active guild is better than solo play for progression. Many guilds are welcoming to new players.

General Leveling Path

Lv 1–10
Novice Training Grounds + Main Quest start. Do not skip this — free gear and EXP.
Lv 10–25
Choose your first job. Follow the Main Quest chain. Prontera fields and Payon area mobs.
Lv 25–40
Continue MQ. Payon Cave, Ant Hell (lv30-35 mobs). Start doing nearby daily quests.
Lv 40–60
Orc area, Byalan Dungeon, Geffen fields. Consider your second job change at Base 50.
Lv 60–75
Second job change (Knight, Hunter, etc.). Memorial Dungeons start becoming accessible.
Lv 75+
Glast Heim, Louyang, Amatsu, Ayothaya areas. Focus on gear and Memorial Dungeons.

Core Game Systems

Ragnarok Zero has several unique progression systems. You don't need to master all of them right away — but knowing they exist helps you plan your character.

Refine System

Weapons and armor can be refined from +0 to +15 using Blacksmith NPCs. Each upgrade increases stats but carries a chance of failure and destruction at higher levels. Use the Refine Simulator to calculate expected costs before spending your zeny.

Activation System (★ Upgrade)

Select weapons and armor can be upgraded from ★ (one-star) to ★★ (two-star) through the Activation System. This requires specific items and significantly boosts the equipment's stats. Check the Activation Guide for full requirements.

Dungeon Enchanting (Affixes)

Memorial Dungeons drop equipment that can be enchanted with Affixes — random stat bonuses from a pool of possible rolls. Understanding which affixes are valuable for your class is key to end-game optimization.

Memorial Dungeons

Instanced dungeons for parties of 5. Each dungeon has a specific theme, mechanics, and rewards. Memorial Dungeons are the primary source of mid and end-game gear upgrades and crafting materials.

Economy Basics

Ragnarok Zero has a fully player-driven economy. Items are bought and sold through player vending stalls in cities (primarily Prontera). There is no auction house — you browse vendors physically or use the search system. Zeny (the in-game currency) is earned primarily through monster drops, quest rewards, and selling to other players.

The most important economic tip: always have a Merchant alt. The Merchant's Overcharge skill lets you sell items to NPCs for 24% more, and Discount lets you buy NPC items for 24% less. At level 1, a Merchant alt is essentially a passive 24% bonus on everything you sell. Create one on day one.

Common Beginner Mistakes

✗ Vendoring everything to NPC
Many drops are worth far more on the player market. Always check prices before selling to an NPC.
✗ Skipping the Main Quest
MQ gives the best EXP in the early game and unlocks important systems. Don't skip it for grinding.
✗ Over-investing in gear early
Gear before level 50-60 becomes obsolete quickly. Save zeny for second-job gear upgrades.
✗ Refining gear too aggressively
High-level refine has a real chance of destroying equipment. Use the Refine Simulator first.
✗ Ignoring elements
Using the wrong element vs. a monster's weakness is a huge DPS loss. Check the monster database before farming.
✗ Not joining Discord/community
RO Zero's meta changes with patches. Being in the community keeps you updated and helps you find parties.
